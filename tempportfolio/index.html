I wish to utilize the multidisciplinary aspect of games and prove how versatile the medium can be.

Below are some of my projects!

echoes - Jelly Beach

echoes is a student-led vertically integrated project at Rochester Institute of Technology. Each year, we examine a theme, and pick apart works from our community to explore how games can foster connections. Our most recent game is Jelly Beach, a child on a boardwalk in the 1960s wants nothing more than a jelly donut!

My role on the Jelly Beach team is the 2D artist/animator and game developer. The majority of the game art and animations was created by me. I've also programmed the player running logic, NPC AI pathfinding script, and the parallax sky effect.

Plinko

Plinko is an academic project that I have adjusted and refined. The aim of this project was to expand my skills in Unity scripting, Components, Collisions, Physics, Particle Systems, Asset Implementation and References.

Rig My Roll

Rig My Roll is a rogue-like slot machine game written in C# and the MonoGame framework. It's a high-level prototype created in a team of four people, the development was constrained to two weeks.

As a project coordinator, I was responsible for designating tasks to team members, and reviewing their work to ensure it met our standards. As a game developer, I designed and programmed the essential logic for the slot machine, manipulating its chances, and contributed to its user interface.

Starlit Wanderer

Starlit Wanderer was created as part of the Granola.gg game jam. The theme was nostalgia, so our game focuses on an astronaut lost in space, and in their final moments, they reminisce on better times.

My role as a game developer was programming the player movement and physics, and designing/implementing a 2D platformer,
